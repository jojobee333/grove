# Research Brief — Meridian Voice Market

**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Slug:** meridian-voice-market  
**Status:** Awaiting approval

---

## Main Research Question

> **Is there a viable, underserved market for an AI writing tool that maintains persistent voice profiles — specifically targeting professional ghostwriters (multi-client voice vaults) and newsletter writers (archive-as-AI) — and what does it look like in concrete terms?**

---

## Background & Context

Meridian is an AI-integrated collaborative workspace (Notion-like) built on pgvector + Claude + Lexical. A strategic pivot is under consideration toward two high-leverage vertical flavors:

1. **Ghostwriters** — one writer managing multiple client voice profiles simultaneously
2. **Newsletter writers** — one writer with a deep personal archive indexed as their AI memory

Both rely on the same underlying technology: multi-profile vector memory + voice fingerprinting. The question is whether the market is real, reachable, and willing to pay before committing to this direction.

---

## Output Use

- Inform a go/no-go product positioning decision for Meridian
- Identify which vertical (ghostwriters vs. newsletter writers) has higher leverage as the entry wedge
- Surface pricing, willingness to pay, acquisition channels, and competitor blind spots
- Used by: sole founder, solo product decision

---

## Constraints

- Focus on 2024–2026 data where available — the AI tooling landscape shifted significantly post-GPT-4
- Prioritize primary-ish signals (community discussions, founder interviews, product reviews, usage data) over generic market reports
- Avoid generic "AI market is $X billion" citations without segment specificity
- Both verticals should be covered — do not collapse to one prematurely
- Solo founder constraints: no enterprise sales, no $100K+ CAC channels

---

## Scope Boundaries

**In scope:**
- Size and growth of the professional ghostwriting market
- Size and growth of the paid newsletter creator market
- Tooling currently used by ghostwriters and newsletter writers
- Evidence of pain around AI voice consistency / style maintenance
- Willingness to pay signals (existing tool pricing, community discussions)
- Competitor blind spots in these verticals
- Acquisition channels that work for bootstrapped tools in the creator economy

**Out of scope:**
- Enterprise / B2B ghostwriting (agencies, PR firms)
- General "AI writing market" sizing
- Technical implementation details
- Monetization modeling (covered in prior analysis)
